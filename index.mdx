DAAAM (Describe Anything, Anywhere, at Any Moment) is a CVPR 2026 research system that brings foundation-model-first scene understanding to robots. It segments every frame with SAM, tracks objects temporally with BotSort and CLIP ReID, and queries the Describe Anything Model (DAM) to produce rich semantic labels — all fed into Hydra to build hierarchical 4D Dynamic Scene Graphs at real-time framerates.

## What DAAAM does

DAAAM processes RGB-D video streams and produces a continuously updated 4D Dynamic Scene Graph that captures **what** objects are, **where** they are in 3D space, and **when** they were observed — enabling downstream tasks like navigation, question answering, and agentic scene understanding.

## Pipeline at a glance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Segment">
    SAM / FastSAM / SAM2 produces per-pixel instance masks on every incoming RGB frame at up to real-time framerates, optionally accelerated with TensorRT.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track">
    BotSort with CLIP ReID maintains temporal identity across frames, accumulating observation history for each tracked object.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign & Ground">
    The assignment worker selects optimal keyframes for each track and sends them to the DAM grounding worker, which returns natural-language semantic labels and embeddings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the scene graph">
    Semantic corrections flow into Hydra to produce a hierarchical 4D Dynamic Scene Graph with objects, places, and rooms — saved to `dsg.json`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  DAAAM requires an NVIDIA GPU with at least 24 GB of VRAM and CUDA 12.x. See [Installation](/installation) for the full prerequisites.
</Note>
